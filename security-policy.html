Security Policy — Timesheet Basic

Last updated: June 18, 2026

1. Scope

This policy applies to Timesheet Basic and related public services used to support the app.

2. Reporting a vulnerability

Please report security issues to security@hoyaqui.es.

Include enough detail to reproduce the issue: affected URL/module, steps, expected result, actual result, and screenshots/logs where possible.

3. Our response commitments

  • Acknowledge receipt within 3 business days.
  • Provide an initial assessment within 7 business days.
  • Share remediation progress for valid reports until closure.

4. Disclosure guidelines

  • Do not publicly disclose vulnerabilities until we confirm remediation.
  • Do not attempt social engineering, data destruction, or service disruption.
  • Test only against systems you own or are authorized to test.

5. Out of scope

  • Issues requiring physical access to devices.
  • Denial-of-service testing or spam.
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party services outside our control.

6. Data encryption

  • All data in transit is encrypted via TLS 1.2+ (enforced by Atlassian Forge).
  • Sensitive data such as API credentials, tokens, and user identifiers are not stored locally by the app; they are processed through Forge's secure context.
  • At rest, worklog data remains in Jira Cloud, which uses Atlassian's data center encryption standards.

7. Access controls

  • Timesheet Basic enforces Jira-native permissions: only users with appropriate Jira access can view/edit worklogs.
  • The app does not create separate authentication layers or bypass Jira RBAC.
  • OAuth scopes are minimized to least-privilege: read:jira-user, read:jira-work, write:jira-work.
  • All API requests use `.asUser()` where possible to maintain user context and authorization checks.

8. Vulnerability management

  • We actively monitor Atlassian Forge platform security advisories and update dependencies.
  • Security patches are deployed to production within 7 days of availability (or sooner for critical CVEs).
  • Vulnerability reports are triaged within 3 business days and shared with relevant customers as public patches.
  • We do not disclose vulnerabilities to the public until patches are deployed.

9. Incident response procedures

  • Security incidents are escalated to security@hoyaqui.es immediately upon discovery.
  • For production incidents, initial triage occurs within 1 business hour.
  • Affected customers are notified within 24 hours of a confirmed breach or data loss.
  • Post-incident, a root cause analysis is performed and remediation steps are documented.
  • Logs of security events are retained for 90 days for forensics and audit purposes.

10. Policy updates

We may update this policy over time. Material changes are reflected by updating the date above.
